Too many words




  • Our thoughts can be disorganized and tangled up. Particularly when worried about tackling an important assignment like the capstone paper


  • This will inevitably lead to disorganized writing


  • This disorganization almost always causes us to use too many words to say things

Eliminate complicated words














  • One of the best ways to make your writing easier on readers is to eliminate complicated words


  • While many people utilize big words to sound more sophisticated, you should use more common synonyms if they exist.
    • Dr. C insists on the K.I.S.S principle
    • the goal is to share ideas, NOT impress

Active voice vs. passive voice


  • In a sentence written in the active voice, the subject of sentence performs the action:


The candidate believes that Congress must place a ceiling on the budget.
12 words = :)


  • In a sentence written in the passive voice, the subject receives the action:


It is believed by the candidate that a ceiling must be placed on the budget by Congress.
17 words = :(

Active voice vs. passive voice


  • Sentences in the passive voice tend to:
    • Use more words
    • Cause a tangle of prepositional phrases (subject often buried next to ‘by’)
    • Confuse the reader by making things more complicated than necessary


Passive: It has been demonstrated by researchers that heart attacks can be caused by high stress.
Active: Researchers have demonstrated that high stress can cause heart attacks.

Passive: The man was bitten by the dog.
Active: The dog bit the man.

Converting sentences to active voice


Passive: It has been demonstrated by researchers that heart attacks can be caused by high stress.

Active: Researchers have demonstrated that high stress can cause heart attacks.



  • Some tips and strategies for converting sentences from the passive to the active voice:
    • Look for a “by” phrase (e.g., “by the dog” in the previous slide). If you find one, the sentence may be in the passive voice
    • Rewrite the sentence so that a subject buried in the “by” clause is closer to the sentence beginning
    • If the subject of the sentence is not clear, see if you can use a general term, such as “researchers,” or “the study,” or “experts in this field.”
    • This sentence ‘flipping’ puts the subject first and often removes unnecessary words

More Pro Tips for active voice


  • Swap ‘ing’ for ‘ed’. Words ending in ‘ing’ tend to be more passive than ‘ed’


  • Go easy on the adverbs. Although it may be more descriptive, too many adverbs slow down your writing and interrupts the flow.
    • If your verb isn’t strong enough (meaning you need to add adverbs), consider changing it.


  • Don’t be afraid to add the ‘person’ into your writing. Science writing is changing! It is now more accepted to write in the first person (e.g., we found…, our results show…).
    • Using the first person humanizes your work and makes it more enjoyable to read for a broader audience.

Pro Tips: Write with the reader in mind


  • Readers do not simply read; they interpret. Any piece of writing, no matter how short, may mean different things to different readers. Choose your words carefully and make sure your writing is suitable for a broad audience
    • beginner,intermediate and advanced scientist in the field


  • Avoid statements like “obviously” or “as everyone knows” at the beginning of sentences.
    • The purpose of science writing is not to establish your intellectual superiority.


  • Never make the reader assume. Be extremely careful with use of pronouns like “it’ or ‘they” in your sentences.
    • You are probably referring to something specific from the previous sentence. However, you could be referring to a certain part of the sentence or something else in the paragraph.
    • In science writing, we spell it out for reader…every single time.
    • Never start a sentence with ‘It’.
